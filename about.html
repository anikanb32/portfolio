About.

Hi (again), I'm Anika! I design with the belief that tech should feel less like a tool and more like a natural extension of how we think, feel, and interact with the world.

As you might have guessed, I really love music. All the way back in middle school, I helped build a Music and Memory program at a local nursing home in Connecticut. We created personalized playlists for residents with dementia, and it was incredible to see how one familiar tune could bring back a whole world of memories. That experience opened my eyes to how powerful emotionally aware design can be.

Since then, I've followed that curiosity through a lot of different lenses: as an immunology researcher fascinated by the brain-body connection, a software engineer building things from the ground up, a computational neuroscience researcher decoding how we think, an AR/VR UX researcher exploring adaptive interfaces, and now, a product designer tying it all together. I love big, messy problems, and I'm always looking for ways to make technology feel a bit more human.
